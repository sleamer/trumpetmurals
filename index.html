Mural Pricing

There are two major variables to consider when planning the price of a mural: Size and Complexity.

Size

A muralâ€™s size is usually dictated by the space available and the aesthetic choice of the client. The price per square foot of the mural raises as the size decreases. Though there is less surface area to paint, the complexity of the art does not change. The same amount of artistic thought to detail, color, and composition are constants regardless of size.


Complexity

There are four possible levels of complexity to a mural. When a bid for a mural is created the difficulty of the project(s) are assessed by Trumpet Murals. This is a rough guideline to help determine where the project falls.


Level Description Example
Level 1: Consists of simple designs and/or backgrounds. It is easy to think of this as accent art.
Level 2: Generic landscapes or spaces with sparse details. Imagine sprawling vistas with distant buildings and/or animals.
Level 3: Specific buildings, close-ups of animals, and/or distant figures. Complex compositions due to space constraints are included in this category (non rectangular spaces)
Level 4: This includes portraits, highly rendered specific animals (pets), highly detailed buildings and complex compositions.

*The prices listed do not include travel, special equipment (scaffolding, scissorlifts), or room and board. These expenses, if needed, will be billed separately as supplies at the end of the project.
